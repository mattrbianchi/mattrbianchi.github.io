Go Is My Spirit Language

July 2016 Â· 2 minute read

When I reveal my love for Go to fellow programmers, I often get inquired as to how this language could make me so passionate and exuberant. My response is usually to take a deep breath in preparation for what, in my mind, can only be an excellent delivery of my thoughts on Go. But what comes next is silence as I realize I don’t know what to lead with, followed by blurting anything that comes to mind like a rambling maniac.

I get raised eyebrows, thoughtful frowns, but the worst is silence. I stop talking as the acknowledgement that I’ve failed swoops over me and I turn my focus to my lunch, maybe asking a question to change the conversation. But now I think I can finally put my finger on it.

Go is my spirit language. It resonates with me. After only a week of learning the syntax, I started to think in Go. In my mind, I became Pocahontas of the computer and I’m painting with all the colors of the wind, my friend.

So, please, come run the hidden pine trails of interfaces. Come taste the sun sweet berries of cross-platform. Come roll in all the riches of multiple threads, and for once… never wonder what they’re worth. The format tool and the compiler are my brothers, the community and the gopher are my friends! And we are all connected to each other, in a circle, in a loop that never ends. How much skill can one grow with Go? If you cut it down, then you’ll never know. And you’ll never lose the worry about unused imports or whether tabs or spaces are better. We need to sing with all the cores of the CPU – need to program with all the colors of the wind. You can keep on coding and still all you’ll know is coding until you can program with all the colors of the wiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiind.

Magically changes into a gopher, kisses you on the forehead, and skips off – yelling back to you: “Try the Tour of Go, you won’t regret it!”
